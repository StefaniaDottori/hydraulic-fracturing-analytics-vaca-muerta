Orgánico
86.4 hrs NPT
Equipment Delay & Communication — 29 events each. Operational, not geological. Highest absolute NPT because it has the most stages (391).
Avg delay 13.3 min/stage. Low screen-out rate (3.8%) — the formation fractures well. NPT is driven by logistics: equipment swaps, inter-stage communication issues.
Higher operational density — more stages, more equipment, more crew interactions. Managing pad logistics and equipment turnaround is the primary lever for improvement.
La Cocina
6.1% screen-out
Screen-out, Rate Drop & Pressure Spike — geologically driven. Highest screen-out count (28). Failure is predictable and formation-specific.
Clay content 24%, quartz <30%, BI 0.38. Ductile rock generates near-wellbore tortuosity → slurry bridges → screen-out. Short PDAT half-length (163 m avg) confirms limited fracture propagation.
Rock mechanics, not operations. Standard frac recipe fails in ductile facies. Requires tailored fluid system, lower pump rates, and smaller mesh proppant — design solution, not field execution solution.
Loma Amarilla
NWB tortuosity #1
Near Wellbore Tortuosity & Pressure Spikes — 17 events each. Carbonate-rich shale with high frac gradient (0.71 psi/ft) generates complex near-wellbore geometry.
BI 0.76 — most brittle formation. High fracture gradient means higher treating pressures and more sensitivity to near-wellbore complexity. Pressure spikes are consistent with fracture reorientation events.
High brittleness enables long fractures (247 m avg half-length) but also creates fracture complexity near-wellbore. Rate ramp-up design and pre-flush optimization can reduce tortuosity events.
Caliza
6.9% screen-out
Screen-out highest rate (6.9%) — 12 events in 173 stages. Carbonate unit above Vaca Muerta s.s. Higher frac gradient and variable lithology create unpredictable pressure response.
Deepest formation tested (~3,260 m TVD avg). Higher treating pressures, more friction, more sensitivity to proppant concentration. PDAT data shows variable half-lengths (127–337 m range).
Limited dataset (173 stages vs 457 La Cocina). Geological properties not yet fully characterized — adding propiedades_geologicas entry for Caliza is priority for robust analysis.

Optimization recommendations
01 La Cocina — frac design redesign
  • Replace standard fluid with lower-viscosity system (slickwater or light hybrid) to reduce near-wellbore friction
  • Reduce pump rate ramp from standard ~80 bpm to ≤60 bpm initial rate — allow fracture to initiate before pressuring up
  • Switch to smaller mesh proppant (100 mesh) for first 30% of volume to avoid bridging in complex fracture geometry
  • Pre-schedule CT standby for La Cocina pads — at 6.1% SO rate, 1–2 events per lateral is statistically expected
02 Screen-out response protocol
  • Data shows "Job terminated" yields highest avg IP (727 bopd) — formalize early termination decision tree for field crews
  • Avoid flush initiation as default response — lowest IP outcome (675 bopd), displaces proppant from fracture face
  • Rate reduction is a valid secondary option — saves stage in ~50% of cases, adds ~20 min NPT on average
  • Document response + outcome per stage to build formation-specific decision database
03 Orgánico — operational efficiency
  • NPT is operational, not geological — focus on Equipment Delay (29 events) and Communication events (29 events)
  • WL-013 (22 events) and WL-025 (16 events): cross-reference with crew rotation logs and service company shift changes
  • Swap-over time tracking (wireline-to-frac transitions) — Corva benchmark shows 23 min/stage savings potential
  • Same pattern in TotalEnergies wells (WL-013, WL-005) — vendor-specific performance review warranted
04 Data gaps to close
  • Add Caliza to propiedades_geologicas.csv — 173 stages (14% of dataset) have no geological property reference
  • Screen-out delay_min = 0 for all events — add actual CT intervention time to event log for true NPT capture
  • Lost Circulation delay also = 0 — likely same data entry gap; actual mud/fluid loss time not being recorded
  • Link pdat_analyst field to events — S. Dottori appears on 173 Caliza stages, enables analyst performance tracking
